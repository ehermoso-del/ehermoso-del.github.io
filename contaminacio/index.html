Air pollution, meteorological, conditions and respiratory infections in Baix Llobregat. A 14-year spatiotemporal analysis.

The objectives of this research project are:

  • Yingqing & Eli are studying all data from Paleja that is a city located in Baix Llobregat county. In this county we are using data from Air Pollution Prevention and Monitoring Network (XPCVA in catalan) corresponding to hourly data of air pollutants from year 1991-2025. Data included: nitrogen oxide (NO),mitrogen dioxid (NO2), nitrogen oxides (NO X), sulfur dioxide(SO2>/sub>)
  • Meteorological data is available from Meteocat XEMA (Meteorological data server of Catalonia), so we choose the nearest meteorological station that is Vallirana. The data structure is base on semi-hourly data that is, data every 30 minutes from 2009. Data included: temperature, relative humidity, wind direction, wind speed, solar and irradiance.
  • Respiratory infection data are collected from Catalonia Information System for Infection Surveillance (SIVIC) using data from regiion 75, that is Barcelona Metropolitan south region 261 is the identificator of the Primary care centre from Vallirana

Air Pollution in Pallejà (2011–2025)

  • The graphs show how the concentrations of nitrogen oxides (NO, NO₂, NOₓ) and sulfur dioxide (SO₂) vary according to hour of the day, month of the year and day of the week. These pollutants are mainly associated with road traffic and combustion processes.

    • Hourly variation

    • The hourly pattern shows a strong morning peak around 7–9 h, when traffic increases during commuting hours. At this moment, NO and NO₂ rise sharply, producing the highest values of NOₓ. After this peak, concentrations decrease during midday, likely due to better atmospheric dispersion caused by higher temperatures and sunlight. A smaller increase appears in the evening, related to the second traffic peak when people return home. SO₂ remains very low and stable during the entire day, indicating that local sources of sulfur emissions are limited.

      • Monthly variation

    • Pollution levels show a clear seasonal cycle. The highest concentrations of nitrogen oxides occur in winter months (December–February). During this period, colder conditions and weaker air circulation can trap pollutants close to the ground. In contrast, concentrations decrease in spring and summer, reaching the lowest values between May and August. Warmer temperatures and stronger atmospheric mixing help disperse pollutants more efficiently. Levels begin to increase again in autumn, as meteorological conditions become more stable.

      • General interpretation and conclusion

      • Overall, the results suggest that road traffic is the main source of nitrogen oxide pollution in Pallejà. The clear peaks during commuting hours and higher levels during weekdays strongly support this idea. Seasonal differences also indicate that meteorological conditions influence air quality, with winter conditions favoring pollutant accumulation and summer conditions improving dispersion. These patterns show that both human activity and weather conditions control air pollution levels, highlighting the importance of reducing traffic emissions and improving urban mobility to protect air quality and public health.

            • Air pollution patterns (NO and NO₂) in 2025

          • The calendars below show the average daily levels of nitrogen oxide (NO) and nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) recorded in Pallejà during 2025. Each mini-calendar corresponds to a different month, and every square represents one day of that month. The color scale reflects the level of air pollution: light yellow shades indicate lower concentrations, while darker orange or red colors represent higher pollution levels.

            • Nitrogen Oxide (NO)

            • The NO calendar indicates that during 2025 most days recorded low to moderate concentrations, although there are some occasional peaks distributed throughout the year. Nitrogen oxide is mainly generated by vehicle traffic and combustion processes, and its concentration changes depending on daily human activity and weather conditions.

                • Analysis

                • Taken together, the two calendars illustrate how air pollution changes day by day during the year. These temporal patterns help identify periods when pollution levels increase and how they may relate to meteorological conditions and respiratory infections analysed in this study. Overall, the results indicate that air pollution follows clear temporal patterns. Higher concentrations of NO and NO₂ tend to appear during certain periods of the year and can be affected by meteorological factors such as wind, atmospheric pressure, or solar radiation. At the same time, the SIVIC data on respiratory infections also show seasonal trends, with higher numbers of illnesses such as bronchiolitis, COVID-19, and influenza, particularly during the winter months. Examining pollution, weather, and health data together helps to better understand when respiratory infections may increase and how environmental conditions could contribute to these patterns.

                    • Meteorological data

                    • Meteorological information was obtained from the XEMA Meteocat network (Meteorological Data Server of Catalonia). The closest meteorological station to the study area is located in Vallirana, which was therefore selected for the analysis. The dataset consists of semi-hourly measurements, meaning that data are recorded every 30 minutes, and the records are available from 2009 onwards. The dataset includes several atmospheric variables, such as temperature, relative humidity, atmospheric pressure, wind direction, wind speed, and solar irradiance. However, in the analysed period the variables corresponding to temperature (X30) and relative humidity (X31) contain missing values, so they were excluded from the descriptive analysis
